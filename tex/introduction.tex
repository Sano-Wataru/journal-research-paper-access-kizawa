%1st paragraph
%
There has been an increasing demand for monitoring physical activity in office environments, such as changes in posture while seated, deskwork movements (e.g., hand movements during PC operation, turning the body), movements to use office equipment (e.g., phones, printers, copiers), movement to another desk for conversation with colleagues, and temporary departures from the office floor (e.g., to restrooms, for lunch breaks), walking to communal areas (e.g., meeting rooms, break rooms).
%
Physical-activity monitoring using smart office technologies has become socially growing awareness, aiming to improve employees' working conditions and enhance overall organizational performance \cite{PAPAGIANNIDIS2020102027}.
%
The global market for smart offices is rapidly expanding, having reached \$31.37 billion in 2020 and projected to reach \$90.63 billion by 2030 \cite{smartofficemaketsize}.

%2nd paragraph
%
Wearable devices are used to measure physical movements, stress, and emotional states of individuals in office environments \cite{6627764,mozos2017stress,ouwerkerk2013wireless}.
%
The wristband-type wearable device DTI-2 has been demonstrated to monitor skin conductance, skin temperature, ambient temperature, ambient light levels, and three-dimensional (3D) acceleration \cite{ouwerkerk2013wireless}.
%
Smart furniture is used to enhance user comfort and functionality in office environments \cite{krejcar2019smart,9216892}.
%
Using smart chairs, various sensors have been integrated to detect seated behaviors and provide real-time feedback, thereby enhancing user comfort \cite{vlaovic2022smart}.
%
RGB cameras are used for continuous monitoring and optimizing the use of office environments \cite{ayers2001monitoring, antonino2019office}.
%
Real-time measurement of office occupancy has been demonstrated using RGB cameras \cite{antonino2019office}.
%
Light-detection-and-ranging (LiDAR) is used to acquire 3D data for recognizing movements of people and objects, particularly indoors \cite{9795869}.
%
The use of multiple LiDAR sensors has been demonstrated to sense indoor spaces without blind spots \cite{oka2021spatial,10159661}.

%3rd paragraph
Zenonos et al.\ presented a data-collection and prediction system aimed at improving employee productivity in office environments \cite{7457166}.
Their study involved using the wearable device Silmee\texttrademark to gather physiological data, such as heart rate and skin temperature, and the smartphone application ``HealthyOffice'' to collect subjective mood data.
They used machine-learning models to predict moods.
They demonstrated their system's effectiveness in a small-scale user study involving the prediction of eight types of moods at five intensity levels.
Yang et al.\ presented a system for real-time detection and tracking of people indoors using multiple cameras \cite{4284740}.
Their research included foreground segmentation, occlusion handling, and tracking handoff between multiple cameras to track the location and movement of individuals from each camera.
Their system was able to detect and track individuals within an office building using 18 cameras, as demonstrated in their experiments.
Behera et al.\ presented a real-time surveillance system for monitoring extensive areas, including indoor environments, using multiple cameras \cite{6409058}.
Their research involved detecting moving objects using background subtraction, object tracking, inter-camera cooperation, and occlusion detection and handling.
Their system was able to detect and track individuals by placing four IP cameras with overlapping fields of view, establishing inter-camera cooperation, as proven from their experiments.

%5th paragraph
% 提案システム
We propose a system that estimates activity levels of people working in office environments using a LiDAR sensor network.
  % 提案システムの目的
  \revised{The system infers activity levels from spatial features extracted from} 3D point-cloud data.
  \begin{revisedblock}
  % 「活動レベル」の定義
  We define activity level as the degree of concentration. 
    %
    The activity level indicates whether a person is focused on the task or has lost concentration.
  % 活動レベルに関する仮説
  We hypothesize the following.
    %
    Concentration is accompanied by active movements.
    %
    The active movements appear as dynamic patterns in the 3D spatial data collected via LiDAR sensors.
  \end{revisedblock}
  % 提案システムのフォーカス
  Our study focuses on developing an activity level estimation system for PC-based deskwork. 
    %
    The deskwork is one of the tasks commonly assumed in office environments.
%
The system collects point-cloud data of people at work. 
  %
  The system converts the collected data to multiple numerical features.
  %
  The features represent 3D characteristics of the data, such as the spatial center of mass.
  %
  The system generates a machine-learning model using the features as input to estimate the activity levels of people during the deskwork.
We conducted evaluation experiments with a PC deskwork scenario to assess the system.
%
We collected point-cloud data of people during \deleted{different lengths of }educational video viewing using a network composed of multiple LiDAR sensors.
%
\deleted{We obtained several indicators representing activity levels of people during work using tests and questionnaires on the educational video content.}
\revised{We obtained several indicators representing the activity levels of people during work using objective evaluation and subjective evaluation of the educational video content.}
We created labels for activity indicators using clustering and generated binary labels on the basis of the consistency of labels among subjects.
We constructed multiple models using different data types and a combination of various feature-extraction methods and evaluated accuracy using the binary labels.

%6th paragraph
We previously presented an edge computing system that estimates activity levels of people working in office environments using a LiDAR sensor network \cite{kizawa2023estimation}.
Differently from the previous study, our study addresses estimation models using multiple features.
The previous study characterized the collected point-cloud data using a single method and a statistical model as the estimation model and using linear correlation as the evaluation metric.
